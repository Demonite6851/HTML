I am almost alturistic unintentionally in certain scenarios, mainly when people are visibly hurt whether its physical or emotional.

I say "alturistic" because I often forget what I'm doing and try to help calm them down. It might land me in hot water down the road, but I can't stand people being unhappy. Even if I don't like them at all, I'd still rather them be smiling than crying.


Independence

Since I am so antisocial, I've grown to love working independently. There is less talking and I usually don't have to rely on anyone else to get my work done. It does mean that I can get off track and distracted easier as there is nobody to keep me focused. But if the environment is quiet enough, that isn't an issue as there are a lot less distractions.

I will always prefer independent work over group work, but I am in no way against it. If I need to work on a team or in a group, I will still try to be helpful to everyone. But sometimes working in a group can be a lot harder for me as sometimes people are lazy and cut corners which leaves other people to pick up the slack. I usually don't care about it, but if other people do, I feel obligated to work more to help alleviate any stress. This can sometimes lead to me being so focused on helping them that I forget about the work I was supposed to be doing.
